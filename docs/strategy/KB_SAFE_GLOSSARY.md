# P2E Inferno Knowledge Base Glossary

> Last Updated: March 10, 2026
> Purpose: A curated glossary for AI knowledge base ingestion. This version keeps user-facing product context, support-safe terminology, and high-value sales context while excluding internal implementation details, security-sensitive material, and developer-only reference data.

## Platform Overview

P2E Inferno is a gamified learning platform focused on helping people build practical skills in Web3 and adjacent frontier technologies through hands-on participation, structured programs, quests, and verifiable achievements.

The platform combines education, guided onboarding, quests, rewards, and community participation. It is designed to move users from curiosity to practical execution by rewarding meaningful actions instead of passive consumption.

## Core Product Concepts

### Bootcamp

A bootcamp is a structured learning program with a defined topic, duration, and curriculum. Bootcamps are used for guided education, usually over multiple weeks, and are intended to help participants build practical skills through completion-based learning.

### Cohort

A cohort is a scheduled run of a bootcamp. A single bootcamp can have multiple cohorts across different dates or seasons. Cohorts define timing, enrollment windows, and participant access for a specific delivery of a program.

### Milestone

A milestone is a major checkpoint inside a cohort or learning program. Milestones break a bootcamp into smaller stages so learners can progress step by step. They usually represent a week, module, or skill cluster and include clear completion requirements.

### Milestone Task

A milestone task is an individual assignment or proof-of-work item inside a milestone. Tasks are the concrete actions learners complete, such as submitting a link, uploading a file, or demonstrating a completed activity.

### Quest

A quest is a guided activity path that encourages action and participation. Quests can be educational, community-driven, or action-based. They are designed to make learning and engagement feel interactive rather than passive.

### Daily Quest

A daily quest is a short recurring quest intended to drive frequent engagement. Daily quests help reinforce habits, encourage consistency, and create a regular rhythm of participation on the platform.

### Check-in

Check-in is the platform's lightweight daily participation action. It is used to reward consistency, maintain streaks, and encourage learners to return regularly.

### Streak

A streak tracks how many consecutive periods a user has successfully checked in or participated. Streaks support habit formation and can be tied to visible progress, status, or rewards.

## Learning and Participation Model

### Learn-by-Doing

The platform is built around practical execution. Users are expected to complete real tasks, submit evidence, and develop working familiarity with tools, protocols, and workflows instead of only reading theory.

### Proof of Work

Proof of Work in this context means evidence that a learner actually completed a required action. This may include a transaction link, published artifact, uploaded screenshot, or other verifiable output tied to a task.

### Completion Criteria

Completion criteria define what a learner must submit or finish for a task or milestone to count as complete. These criteria are used to keep programs measurable and fair.

### Reward

Rewards are incentives tied to successful participation or verified completion. They are meant to reinforce useful action, not just attendance or passive presence.

### xDG

xDG is the platform's branded term for the in-app earned progression value that may also be referred to internally as XP. It is earned through participation and completion and is used as a visible measure of progress and engagement inside the platform.

## Identity, Access, and Verification

### Wallet

A wallet is the user's onchain identity and transaction tool. It allows participation in blockchain-based actions such as proving activity, interacting with protocols, and receiving certain types of rewards or access.

### GoodDollar Verification

GoodDollar verification is used as a human-verification layer for some platform experiences. Its role is to help reduce abuse, improve trust, and support fair participation by distinguishing real users from low-quality or automated activity.

### Attestation

An attestation is a verifiable record associated with a user action, achievement, or status. In the platform context, attestations can be used to represent completion, recognition, or proof that an important milestone was reached.

### Certificate

A certificate is a formal completion artifact for a learner who finishes a program or meets the required conditions. It is intended to represent a meaningful achievement rather than a simple participation badge.

## Payments and Access

### Fiat Payment

Fiat payment refers to paying with traditional currency through supported checkout flows. This is typically the easier option for users who are newer to onchain systems.

### Crypto Payment

Crypto payment refers to paying through blockchain-based assets and wallet transactions. This is typically suited to users who are already comfortable with onchain interaction.

### Subscription

A subscription is a recurring or renewable access model used for some parts of the platform. It can be tied to continued access, premium features, or participation eligibility.

### Unlock Protocol

Unlock Protocol is part of the platform's access and membership model. It supports tokenized access, membership-like flows, and onchain gating for certain experiences.

### Lock

A lock is a tokenized access mechanism used to manage entry, membership, or eligibility for a specific experience. In practice, it is part of how the platform can represent access in an onchain-native way.

## Ecosystem and Engagement Concepts

### DG Token

DG is the platform's reward and ecosystem token concept. It appears in learning, participation, and certain access or incentive flows. In user-facing terms, it represents earned value inside the broader platform economy.

### DG Token Vendor

The DG Token Vendor is the platform's token interaction layer. It is part of how users engage with the DG ecosystem through guided, product-defined actions.

### Onchain Action

An onchain action is any user activity that results in a blockchain transaction or a verifiable blockchain state change. In the platform, these actions are often used as practical learning steps.

### Community Activation

Community activation refers to turning passive members into active participants through structured onboarding, challenges, events, and progress-driven experiences.

## Programs and Offerings

### Education Services

Education services are structured learning offerings such as workshops, guided programs, and bootcamps intended to help users or communities build practical onchain skills.

### Onboarding Services

Onboarding services help users or communities move from confusion to confident participation. They focus on first steps, tool familiarity, and clear guided progression.

### Events and Activations

Events and activations are engagement formats such as tournaments, challenges, showcases, or coordinated community experiences designed to drive participation and visibility.

### Infernal Sparks

Infernal Sparks is a flagship bootcamp-style program focused on learn-by-doing Web3 onboarding. It is used as a concrete example of the platform's broader educational model: structured progression, practical tasks, and reward-backed participation.

## Support-Safe Operational Terms

### Enrollment

Enrollment is the process by which a user joins a cohort or program. It may include registration, qualification, payment, or access confirmation depending on the experience.

### Submission

A submission is the evidence a learner provides to complete a task or milestone. This may be a file upload, a URL, a transaction reference, or another acceptable proof format.

### Verification

Verification is the review or confirmation process used to determine whether a submission satisfies the requirements of a task, quest, or milestone.

### Completion Status

Completion status describes whether a user has fully satisfied the conditions for a given task, milestone, quest, or program.
